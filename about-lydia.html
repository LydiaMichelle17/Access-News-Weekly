Founder of Access News Weekly

Lydia Michelle Morales

M.A. Special Education | M.A. Educational Therapy | B.A. Child and Adolescent Development

Lydia Michelle Morales is an educator, educational therapist, tutor, behavioral therapist, and special education advocate with extensive experience supporting children and students with diverse learning needs. Her background includes early childhood development, special education, educational therapy, autism support, tutoring, curriculum development, parent collaboration, and individualized student support.

Throughout her career, Lydia has worked closely with children from preschool through school age in classroom, home, and one-on-one learning environments. Her professional experience includes serving as a preschool teacher, teacher assistant, behavioral therapist, personal aide, and private tutor. In these roles, she has supported students with homework, academic goals, developmental needs, behavior plans, IEP-related services, and social-emotional growth. She has also collaborated closely with parents, teachers, therapists, and school teams to help students build confidence and make meaningful progress.

Lydia founded Access News Weekly to help make current events accessible, age-appropriate, and meaningful for every learner. Drawing from her years of experience working with students in classrooms, homes, and one-on-one educational settings, she creates learning tools that support comprehension, confidence, communication, and inclusion.

Her work is rooted in the belief that every student deserves access to real-world information in a way they can understand. Through Access News Weekly, Lydia's mission is to help every learner connect with the world, participate in meaningful conversations, and build confidence through accessible current events.

Access with dignity.

Access News Weekly is built around age-appropriate current events, predictable routines, and practical classroom tools that help students understand more and connect more.
