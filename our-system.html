The Access News Weekly system

The world, made understandable.

Access News Weekly is a weekly current-events curriculum for special education classrooms and inclusive learning environments. It helps teachers turn timely news into accessible reading, measurable academic practice, and real-world conversation.

Executive summary

A practical bridge between school, family, community, and civic life.

Current events are one of the clearest bridges between school, family, community, and civic life. Students talk about what is happening in the world at lunch, in class discussions, at home, and in the community. Yet many students with disabilities are left out of these conversations because the news is often too fast, too abstract, too text-heavy, too emotionally intense, or too difficult to adapt quickly.

Access News Weekly is designed for students who need additional support with reading, language, attention, processing, communication, or social-pragmatic skills. It is especially useful for students with autism, intellectual disabilities, learning disabilities, language disorders, processing differences, emerging literacy skills, and English language needs.

The purpose is simple: help every student understand the same world their peers are discussing.

Product promise

Access News Weekly helps special education teachers turn current events into accessible reading, academic practice, and real-world conversation every Friday.

The challenge

The accessibility gap in current events.

Standard news sources often create barriers rather than access. Even simplified news products may still include complex sentence structures, abstract concepts, idioms, sensory-heavy media, fast pacing, mature emotional content, or assumptions about background knowledge that some students have not yet developed.

Teachers adapt alone

Teachers often have to choose safe stories, remove overwhelming details, rewrite text at multiple levels, simplify vocabulary, create questions, and prepare alternate activities.

Students miss shared topics

When materials are inaccessible, students may miss the same current-events conversations happening around them.

Materials must feel appropriate

Older students who read at lower levels still deserve content that feels mature, respectful, and connected to the real world.

The solution

The Friday Five

Each Friday issue includes five accessible news stories in a consistent format. The topics change, but the structure stays familiar so students can spend more energy on comprehension, vocabulary, and discussion.

What teachers receive

Teacher-ready materials every week.

  • Five current-events stories
  • Three reading levels for each story
  • Vocabulary words and definitions by level
  • Comprehension questions and answer choices
  • Social Passport conversation prompts
  • Teacher notes and sensory-aware preview notes when needed
  • Print-friendly classroom pages
  • Optional home conversation prompt

Classroom uses

Flexible enough for real classrooms.

Teachers can use Access News Weekly for whole-class lessons, small-group reading, speech-language sessions, morning meeting, transition programs, inclusion support, English learner support, home conversation practice, and IEP-aligned progress monitoring support.

The product is not just a simplified news feed. It is a classroom resource built around access, dignity, predictability, communication, and teacher usability.

Three levels. One shared topic.

Different access points for the same classroom conversation.

The goal is not to create separate topics for separate students. The goal is to create one shared topic with multiple access points.

Level 1: Access

Designed for students with emerging literacy, significant language needs, AAC users, or students requiring highly concrete text.

  • Very short sentences
  • Core nouns and verbs
  • One key vocabulary word
  • Concrete comprehension checks
  • Visual or symbol support in future classroom materials

Level 2: Instructional

Designed for students who benefit from plain language, concrete facts, direct vocabulary instruction, and clear cause-and-effect structure.

  • Simple paragraphs
  • Two vocabulary words
  • Clear sequence
  • Simple multiple-choice questions
  • Direct teacher support

Level 3: Independent

Designed for students ready for fuller accessible nonfiction, more context, and supported discussion.

  • Longer accessible passages
  • Three vocabulary words
  • Background information
  • Why-it-matters context
  • Critical thinking support

Educational philosophy

News is more than information. News is social currency.

Students use shared knowledge to join conversations, ask questions, build relationships, and understand the world around them. When students cannot access current events, they can be left out of everyday conversations at home, at school, and in the community.

Social Passport

Each story includes a simple conversation starter, such as “Did you know scientists found a new planet?” For some students, starting a conversation is harder than answering a question.

Routine as a scaffold

Predictability is access. The Friday Five structure gives students the same categories in the same order each week, reducing cognitive load.

Access without talking down

Access News Weekly adapts the complexity of the language, not the dignity of the student. The content should be understandable without being childish.

Sensory-aware and trauma-informed

The news can be frightening. Presentation matters.

Access News Weekly is not designed to avoid every difficult topic, but it is designed to present information with care. When a difficult topic must be addressed, the focus should be factual, concrete, and oriented toward helpers, solutions, safety, or civic understanding.

  • Avoid graphic or sensationalized details
  • Reduce unnecessary fear
  • Watch for sensory triggers
  • Provide teacher preview notes for sensitive topics
  • Preserve the dignity and maturity of older students

Universal Design for Learning

Designed to reduce predictable barriers before students encounter them.

Access News Weekly supports learner agency, belonging, relevance, access, and flexible expression. Supports that are essential for some students can be useful for many.

Learning barrier Access News Weekly response
Student cannot decode grade-level news Same story is available at three reading levels
Student understands better with visuals Level 1 supports visual and symbol-based access in classroom materials

IEP-aligned support

Useful classroom evidence without overclaiming.

Access News Weekly should not be described as guaranteeing IEP compliance. IEP decisions remain the responsibility of the IEP team, educators, families, and the district.

A stronger and more accurate claim is that Access News Weekly provides IEP-aligned progress monitoring support. The curriculum gives teachers consistent weekly opportunities to observe and document student performance in areas commonly addressed in IEPs.

Possible goal areas

  • Nonfiction comprehension
  • Vocabulary
  • Expressive language
  • Receptive language
  • Social-pragmatic language
  • AAC or multimodal communication

Privacy and accessibility

Built for a teacher-facing classroom resource.

The first version is designed to be privacy-conscious and simple to use. Teachers can view, print, and use weekly issues without entering student names, IEP documents, disability categories, or personally identifiable student information.

Privacy-conscious

No student accounts, no student names, no IEP records, no disability categories, and no student quiz-response storage in the demo version.

Accessibility-first

The website and curriculum experience should support readable typography, strong contrast, semantic structure, keyboard navigation, and print-friendly pages.

Human-reviewed

Content should be reviewed for factual accuracy, clarity, tone, classroom usability, sensory concerns, and age-appropriate presentation before publication.

Outcomes to measure

What success can look like in the classroom.

Access News Weekly is designed to support practical classroom outcomes teachers can observe over time.

Comprehension

Students identify main ideas, details, and basic who/what/where/why information.

Vocabulary

Students learn and use weekly words connected to real-world topics.

Communication

Students retell facts, answer questions, or use Social Passport prompts.

Confidence

Students participate in shared topics with peers at their own reading level.

Connection

Students bring classroom learning into conversations at home and in the community.

Why it matters

Access News Weekly is built on a simple idea: students deserve access to the world at a level they can understand. The goal is to help a student say, “I know what happened. I can tell you about it. I have something to say. I am part of the conversation.”
