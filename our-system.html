The Access News Weekly system

The world, made understandable.

Access News Weekly is a weekly current-events curriculum for special education classrooms and inclusive learning environments. It helps teachers turn timely news into accessible reading, academic practice, and real-world conversation.

View sample Friday Five Book a meeting

The challenge

Current events can be a bridge, but many students are left out.

Students hear about the world at lunch, at home, in class, and in the community. Standard news is often too fast, abstract, text-heavy, emotionally intense, or difficult to adapt quickly. Teachers often have to rewrite articles, simplify vocabulary, create questions, preview sensitive topics, and build alternate activities on their own.

Too much adaptation time

Teachers need safe, clear, classroom-ready materials without spending hours rewriting the news each week.

Mixed reading levels

One classroom may include emerging readers, students working at an instructional level, and students ready for fuller context.

Conversation matters

Students need more than facts. They need language they can use to join real conversations.

The solution

The Friday Five

Each weekly issue includes five accessible news stories in a consistent format. The topics change, but the routine stays familiar so students can spend more energy on comprehension, vocabulary, and discussion.

Three levels. One shared topic.

Different access points for the same classroom conversation.

Level 1: Access

Short, concrete sentences for students with emerging literacy, significant language needs, AAC users, or students who benefit from highly concrete text.

  • Simple syntax
  • Core nouns and verbs
  • One key vocabulary word
  • Concrete comprehension checks

Level 2: Instructional

Clear short paragraphs for students who benefit from plain language, direct vocabulary instruction, and explicit cause-and-effect structure.

  • Plain English
  • Two vocabulary words
  • Simple multiple-choice questions
  • Direct teacher support

Level 3: Independent

Longer accessible nonfiction passages with background information, academic vocabulary, and “why it matters” context.

  • Fuller context
  • Three vocabulary words
  • Discussion-ready ideas
  • Critical thinking support

What teachers receive

Teacher-ready materials every week.

  • Five current-events stories
  • Three reading levels for each story
  • Vocabulary words and definitions by level
  • Comprehension questions and answer choices
  • Social Passport conversation prompts
  • Teacher notes and sensory-aware preview notes when needed
  • Print-friendly classroom pages

Classroom uses

Built for flexible instruction.

Teachers can use Access News Weekly for whole-class lessons, small-group reading, speech-language sessions, morning meeting, transition programs, inclusion support, English learner support, home conversation practice, and IEP-aligned progress monitoring support.

Design commitments

Access without talking down.

Access

Communication

Prompts help students participate in real conversations.

Usability

Materials are designed to be practical for busy teachers.

Safety and review

Human-reviewed, sensory-aware, and classroom-conscious.

Access News Weekly is designed to make the world understandable without overwhelming students. Difficult topics are handled with care, focusing on facts, helpers, solutions, safety, and concrete understanding.

  • No graphic or sensationalized details
  • Teacher preview notes for sensitive topics
  • No student accounts required for the demo version
  • No student names, IEP records, or disability categories collected
  • Privacy-conscious and accessibility-first design

Why it matters

Access News Weekly is built on a simple idea: students deserve access to the world at a level they can understand. The goal is to help a student say, “I know what happened. I can tell you about it. I have something to say. I am part of the conversation.”
