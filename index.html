The accessibility gap

News moves fast. Classrooms need access.

Many students are left out of current-events conversations because news is often too abstract, too text-heavy, too fast, or too emotionally intense. Access News Weekly turns timely topics into predictable, classroom-ready reading and discussion.

The instructional system

Same stories. Different levels. One classroom conversation.

Each issue gives teachers a practical way to teach current events across mixed reading levels without creating separate lessons from scratch.

  • Five current-events stories every week
  • Three reading levels for each story
  • Vocabulary words and comprehension checks
  • Teacher notes and sensory-aware preview notes
  • Printable classroom-friendly issue pages
Learn more about Access News Weekly

Three reading levels

Designed for access without talking down.

Level 1: Access

Short, concrete sentences for emerging readers, students who use AAC, and learners who need strong visual or language support.

Level 2: Instructional

Plain English, clear cause and effect, and embedded vocabulary for growing readers and literal thinkers.

Level 3: Independent

Richer context and academic vocabulary for students ready for supported nonfiction reading and discussion.
